Thus begins the Year of the Buckwheat

Dear readers,

That would be the genus Eriogonum to those of you in the know, but the official name must start with a B since the 1st year of this blog was christened the Year of the Astragalus. Next year can be the Year of Compositae. Perhaps the Daleas in 2009?

It is official, I will be running a Utah Seeds of Success team again this year. I am excited, but field work is a long ways off. Today I went to work at the GIS lab, which tragically has not a single window to the outdoors, and I still found my brow dripping with perspiration because I was fantasizing on wandering through the desert, squeezing swelling buds, watching corollas part their lips to the desert breeze, nectar glistening in the morning sun, insects weaving drunkenly through the air laden with pollen grains. I am in an ecstatic dreamlike state. A transcendental walking meditation on native plants. And who will I wander the fields of flowers with this year? Maybe you dear reader... I am looking for a crack team of volunteers who are aroused by plant reproduction, the conservation thereof, and good human comeraderie in the wild west. Contact me if you can.

Here is the teaser for the next post: Its not even close to spring yet and most plants will be lying patiently dormant under a blanket of snow for at least a few months yet, so I will have to content myself with dreaming and doing research. To start, I'll be doing a little reading on Eriogonum - one of Utah's most diverse and beautiful plant genera. At this address you will soon find a brief treatise on the evolution and phylogeny of this group of plants to whet your whistle for the field season.

Yours, etc...

Greg
