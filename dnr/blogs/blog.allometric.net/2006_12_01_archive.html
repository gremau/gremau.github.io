Tuesday, December 19, 2006

not eating him

once, in another country, i ate a dog. i didn't mean to order it, but the menu was in a different language and I accidentally pointed to a dish of noodles stirfried with pieces of a dog. i ate the pieces of meat not knowing that it was a dog until i saw the golden brown roast dog laying on a cutting board in the middle of the restaurant. the dog had gleaming white teeth and pieces of meat carved from its sides. i looked down at the meat on my plate and thought about my own dog laying on the couch in front of the tv. i ate some of the noodles with a dog. but i would never eat my dog. i love my dog and i would never eat him. the roast dog that i ate tasted okay, but i will never eat a dog again. sometimes when i am watching tv and my dog is sitting next to me on the couch i say to myself that i will never eat him. i tell him. when i am cooking a vegetarian dish in the kitchen and he sits by my feet and begs, i am not thinking about cooking him. he just sits there next to the oven watches me cook, and i am not thinking about putting him in the oven. i will always love the dog and never eat him. i am not in another country where it might be socially acceptable to eat my dog. when i am watching tv with my dog and that commercial featuring that one dog comes on i try to get my dog to watch and see the other dog and recognize that it is a dog, but he just looks at me and puts his head back down on the couch and closes his eyes. but i recognize the dog. that other dog on tv. not my dog.

Spitting blood (while revisiting Hanoi)

1am
On the third cup the cafe sua da starts to act like an amphetamine. My hindbrain is resonating and I can see hot bones glowing under the golden-brown skin of the two mongrels on the chopping block. Their lips have curled away from their ivory teeth into an ironic grin. Please God don't let this be their savory flesh between my teeth. It's definitely not beef.

2am
"Stop whining, you'll never get your change back. Free market or no, this is still a communist country. Gimme that bag." The betel nuts are wrapped in fresh tobacco with a smear of white lime inside. "Chew it a little and cram the pulp between your gum and lips." She tells me it feels like her belly is filling up with warm sloshing blood and that it's good, calming. We spit crimson streams in unison.

3am
This guy that looks like a Vietnamese David Bowie grins at us and slaps his forearm with his fingers. The glittery polish on his nails sparkles with the light form the sodium lamps above us on the overpass . Its a question. I look over and smile "Do we want that?" Keiko shakes her half-Japanese head, makes a frowny face. We just sit there on the curb, kicking the dust around a little while David Bowie and his friend examine us. Eventually, the man comes back on his Honda Dream and says he can't find any. We turn David Bowie down again and then leave. The olive drab driver that was sleeping in his cyclo when we passed a half hour ago whistles at us as we pass. He looks like he was in the war and he can conjure smoke from the seat of his ancient cyclo. There is a handoff behind the garbage truck.

4am
"My take on it is this...passion is consuming, like a flame. It burns until there is nothing left to burn, and then youre both left standing there cold and bewildered, wondering how to get warm again."

"Maybe thats true" I say, "but what about just wanting company?" We kick our legs around in the muggy air between the windowsill and the street, quiet for a while. "This shit tastes like the newspaper it came in"

"Yeah, lets smoke the rest."

The last two shirtless men are talking in soft, sleepy voices down on the street as their tired daughters stack the plastic stools together and carry them inside. This morning it will be re-election day, and we are watching a worker slowly make his way down the street putting flags up on the power poles.

Thursday, December 07, 2006

the desert

It was a big piece of undeveloped land across the creek behind our house. There were cottonwoods by the pond, clumps of scrub oak in the dry areas, and then big piles of rock and construction debris in between. During flood season huge bulldozers and shovels would come down and push the granite river rocks around and dredge all the mud out of the rapidly filling up pond. Still, half our yard got washed away one year. You could see the tracks that those big machines left in the ground for years afterward. That was where the Nunnelly boys hid the first Playboy I ever saw. Kirk and Clayton took me down there to look at it one afternoon - it was hidden in a clump of oaks across the creek where you couldn't see any houses. Same place we would go to turn over rocks and find scorpions and shoot at magpies with the bow and arrow. It was the best place for porno, scorpions, and shooting things that we had in our neigborhood. One winter there was a family of coyotes that lived over there for a few weeks. I would wake up early and watch them skulking around across the creek at dawn, and I could hear them howling from my bed. Sometimes I would imagine the coyotes walking through my yard in the middle of the night, just on the other side of the wall a few feet from where I was trying to sleep. We used to walk up along the creek to a place called Big Rock. It was in the hollow below Alta high school and we thought there would be hippies drinking beer and smoking cigarettes up there. One winter we even went up there and walked right on the frozen creek. It was sunny and you could skate across the icy parts to the rocky parts, and then push off and slide to the next rocky part. In some places the ice was brittle and crumbly and would shatter into sugary powder when you hit it. Clay dunked his foot in the water underneath, but we kept going to Big Rock. There were never any hippies there. Just a big rock covered with grafitti and the dirt was worn all fine and dusty like lots of people had been walking around there before us, looking for things to do.
