This page lists publicly available data from various research sites, weather stations, etc. that I've worked on at some point. Email me or check the links below if you have questions.

AmeriFlux NMEG cluster

There are six AmeriFlux research sites in this cluster. These sites have been running for 7 or more years. Currently our most accessible public data has reached the level 2 QA stage with AmeriFlux and links to those files are below. For more detailed information or data contact me or Marcy Litvak.

  • Mountainair Pinyon-Juniper (US-Mpj): site info, level 2 data
  • Sevilleta Grassland (US-Seg): site info, level 2 data
  • Sevilleta Shrubland (US-Ses): site info, level 2 data
  • Valles Caldera Mixed Conifer (US-Vcm): site info, level 2 data
  • Valles Caldera Ponderosa Pine (US-Vcp): site info, level 2 data
  • Willard Juniper Savannah (US-Wjs): site info, level 2 data

Hidden Canyon

A weather station and soil sensors network in operation from 2009-2013 in the Wasatch Range in Utah. For more info see the wiki pages. The data below are corrected, gap filled datasets being developed for a modeling study. So... they are very provisional and not all sensors are included.

  • 2010, 2011, 2012

Red Butte Canyon weather stations

There are four old stations in the Red Butte Canyon research natural area near the University of Utah campus. For older data and more information on these stations see the official RBC data page and the wiki pages.

  • Daily data from all 4 weather stations. Current to Jan 15th, 2014.
  • 10-minute data from weather station 1. Current to Jan 11th, 2014.

Rio Mesa weather stations

Two weather stations are located at the U of U Rio Mesa field station. The stations have been in operation since June 2006 and these data are current to July 2013.

  • Daily data and hourly data from the station near the gauge on the Dolores River (WS1).
  • Daily data and hourly data from the station at the mouth of Line Canyon (WS2).
