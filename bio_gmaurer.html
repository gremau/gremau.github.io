Greg Maurer is a broadly-trained ecologist and environmental data scientist who specializes in applying modern, data-intensive methods to understand Earth’s changing ecosystems and inform critical resource management and conservation efforts. His primary research interests are in the biogeochemistry and ecohydrology of drylands, particularly the effects of climate variability, global change, and disturbance on vegetation and soil systems. As a data scientist he is skilled at synthesizing and analyzing complex, real-world data from diverse sources with the end goal of creating, communicating and publishing impactful research products.

Greg earned a Ph.D. in Ecology from the University of Utah in 2014, gained postdoctoral experience at the University of New Mexico and University of California, Berkeley, and now holds a position as a Data Scientist and Information Manager with the Jornada Basin LTER program at New Mexico State University. He wears many professional hats, including data analyst, data manager, co-PI, programmer, instructor, student mentor, and working-group leader. He enjoys collaborating with diverse and inclusive teams, is technologically savvy, and approaches his work with an open-science philosophy.
